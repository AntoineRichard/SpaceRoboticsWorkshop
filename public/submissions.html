Submissions

The SMC-IT/SCC 2024 Technical Committee is seeking three kinds of submissions at this time: full papers, presentations, and workshop proposals.

Full papers with presentation (both SMC-IT and SCC)

SMC-IT/SCC 2024 will again use a single-pass, full-paper review process. Full papers can be up to 10 pages in length and require a verbal presentation. Authors of full papers must submit a final version of their paper of up to 10 pages, not including references.

All papers accepted for SMC-IT/SCC 2024 will be published in the IEEE conference proceedings, indexed in the IEEE Xplore database. Note that IEEE has a "Podium and Publish" policy for conferences, which means that no manuscript will be published in IEEE Xplore without first being presented at the conference. Some selected papers may be invited to appear in a special issue of a reputable journal in the field.

Presentations without accompanying papers (only SCC)

Authors of presentations which will not have a corresponding paper need to submit a 1-page abstract, which will allow the conference organizers to determine if the proposed presentation is germane for the conference, determine which track/session the proposed presentation belongs, and ensure the author is in contact with the track/session chair for feedback prior to the final submission. All presentation-only abstract submissions must be submitted by the full paper deadline.

All accepted presentations will be distributed to the conference attendees, with the consent of the author and their organization, but will not be published in the IEEE Xplore database.

Workshop submissions (both SMC-IT and SCC)

In SMC-IT/SCC 2024, we will maintain the successful workshop session setup, delving deeper into specific emerging technology topics.

If you wish to propose a workshop, please provide a 1-2 page abstract detailing your workshop's theme, scope, and objectives. Include information about potential speakers you have in mind. Additionally, make sure your proposal clearly outlines the workshop format, indicating if it will involve presentations, papers, posters, working sessions, or a mix of these elements. Also, specify whether you prefer a full-day workshop or one/two half-day slots. You can find links to the submission site and a workshop proposal template below.
