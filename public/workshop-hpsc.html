HPSC – Redefine What’s Possible for the Future of Space Computing

IEEE Space Mission Challenges for Information Technology - IEEE Space Computing Conference
Computer History Museum  •  Mountain View, CA, USA  •  15-19 July 2024

Summary

Advanced edge computing is the cornerstone of future space missions where vast amounts of data collected by sensors and imaging devices need to be processed in situ to enable autonomous decision-making. High-Performance Spaceflight Computing (HPSC) processor, being developed under a public-private partnership between Microchip and NASA/JPL, represents the most cutting-edge space computing solution that brings AI acceleration to a space processor for the first time.

This workshop will begin with a keynote address by NASA executive Dr. Prasun Desai on the agency’s strategic investment in HPSC technology. Both Microchip and JPL will give an overview of the HPSC processor from hardware and software perspectives. Esteemed guest speakers from the aerospace industry will share their insights on why the HPSC is a game changer for space computing. Following the presentations, a Q&A session will be held, offering attendees the opportunity to engage with the expert panel.
