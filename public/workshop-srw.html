SPACE ROBOTICS WORKSHOP

IEEE Space Mission Challenges for Information Technology - IEEE Space Computing Conference
Computer History Museum  •  Mountain View, CA, USA  •  15-19 July 2024

Summary

The "Space Robotics" Workshop is a two-day in-person seminar within the 2024 IEEE International Conference on Space Mission Challenges for Information Technology (SMC-IT), featuring tutorials, panel discussions and invited presentations on the use of AI and novel software elements in robotics for space. industry.

The field of space robotics is entering a transformative era marked by an array of ambitious missions and diverse collaborations. This era is characterized by significant advancements in robotic missions to celestial bodies such as the Moon, Mars, and beyond – exemplified by programs like NASA's VIPER and Artemis campaign, Mars Sample Return, Psyche, and future explorations of Jupiter's icy moons like Europa, as well as Saturn’s moons such as Enceladus and Titan. Further propelling this advancement is the collaboration between NASA, commercial entities through initiatives like the CLPS missions, and international partners, all converging to support these ambitious endeavors.

In this context, the scope of applications for space robotics is expanding exponentially. Robots, varying in design and function, are being tasked with a spectrum of critical roles – from station keeping and in-space servicing to intricate assembly, manufacturing, and the utilization of in-situ resources (ISRU) for sustainable operations. Moreover, comprehensive exploration missions to lunar and planetary surfaces are becoming increasingly reliant on these robotic systems. This new era emphasizes the growing need for a significant shift towards greater autonomy in space robotics. The reliance on human oversight is diminishing, paving the way for the rise of sophisticated, data-driven autonomous systems, primarily powered by Artificial Intelligence (AI). These AI systems, essential for autonomous operations, demand extensive and varied datasets for training and validation as well as realistic and robust simulators. This need presents both a formidable challenge and a unique opportunity in advancing space robotics.

Our workshop is designed to address the broad spectrum of requirements and challenges presented by these diverse applications by bringing together experts from terrestrial robotics, space robotics, and flight software. We will discuss the unique demands of a variety of use cases, identifying how they can benefit from shared technological solutions and unified frameworks. Through collaborative dialogue, the workshop will explore current developments, pinpoint critical challenges, and discuss the research that is needed to enable future robotics and autonomy envision the future needs and missions in space robotics. Emphasizing the critical role of autonomy, we will outline strategies to enable these complex, multi-faceted robotic systems to operate more independently and effectively in the dynamic environment of space.

The Workshop will be chaired by Ignacio G. Lopez-Francos (NASA Ames Research Center / KBR), Meera Day Towler (Southwest Research Institute, Space Robotics Program Manager -- Intelligent Systems Division), Dr Antoine Richard (University of Luxembourg -- Space Robotics Group).

The Workshop format will include invited presentations, panel discussions, and tutorials.
