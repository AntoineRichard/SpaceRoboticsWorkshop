Balancing Performance, Fault-tolerance, and Security for Future Space Systems: What are the trends? What are our challenges?

IEEE Space Mission Challenges for Information Technology - IEEE Space Computing Conference
Computer History Museum  •  Mountain View, CA, USA  •  15-19 July 2024

Purpose

Reliability/availability/security and fault tolerance drive the trade-space for system architecture development. However, in conferences, they are often discussed in separate sessions. When these topics are discussed separately:

  • Codependent components and realistic methods for implementation are overlooked.
  • Optimization can be misguided.
  • Mitigation can be inaccurately applied.
The intent of this workshop is to discuss the system development trade-space in one forum to capture the realistic challenges that design teams are required to manage.

Workshop Description

This workshop will provide a glimpse into the development and assurance of a space mission aimed to support advanced data handling. It will focus on how each of the system’s complex components and their susceptibilities drive the product’s trade-space from a system-architect’s and mission assurance perspective.

  • We will hear from NASA and ESA regarding their perspectives on the future of advanced data handling in space applications.
  • We will then discuss how radiation, reliability, security, and mitigation present real challenges for developing complex space architectures targeted for advanced data handling.
  • System architects will provide their perspectives on the trade space for managing radiation, reliability/availability, and security requirements:
    • How do system requirements drive development?
    • How does trade affect theoretical expectations (realistic limitations vs. initial concepts)?
  • Mission assurance will follow the system architecture presentation; discussing how to verify function/operations, reliability/availability/security, and fault tolerance meet system requirements.
  • The workshop will end with a panel discussion addressing known challenges, misinterpretations of said challenges, and other concepts/challenges that are currently not being considered (or overlooked) – i.e., what are we missing and are there better ideas?

The Workshop is chaired by Melanie Berg (Space R3 LLC), Wes Powell (NASA/GSFC), and Jim Butler (NASA/JPL).
